    Explore Jharkhand

    The Mountains

    Jharkhand is home to several picturesque mountains and hills, forming part of the Chotanagpur Plateau. These mountains are rich in minerals, dense forests, and tribal culture. Prominent ranges include the "Rajmahal Hills", "Parasnath Hills"(the highest peak in Jharkhand and a Jain pilgrimage site), and "Netarhat Hills", known as the "Queen of Chotanagpur" for their scenic beauty. These ranges not only support diverse flora and fauna but also hold spiritual and cultural significance.

    Wild Life

    Jharkhand, often referred to as the "land of forests".Jharkhand is richly endowed with forests and wildlife, covering about 30% of its land area. The state's dense forests are part of the Chotanagpur Plateau and are home to a wide variety of flora and fauna. Major wildlife species include elephants, tigers, leopards, deer, sloth bears, and many species of birds and reptiles. Prominent wildlife sanctuaries and national parks like Betla National Park, Dalma Wildlife Sanctuary, and Palamau Tiger Reserve protect these ecosystems.

    Religious Tourism

    Jharkhand is home to numerous temples that reflect its rich spiritual and cultural heritage. The most famous among them is the Baidyanath Dham Temple in Deoghar, one of the twelve Jyotirlingas of Lord Shiva and a major pilgrimage site for Hindus.There are many other temples like Parasnath Temple (Shikharji),Rajrappa Temple,Maa Tara Chandi Temple,Jagannath Temple (Ranchi),Pahari Mandir (Ranchi),Sun Temple (Bundu),Deori Mandir,Bindudham (Binduwasini Temple) etc.

    Adventure

    Jharkhand offers a range of adventure activities for nature and thrill seekers, thanks to its rugged terrain, dense forests, waterfalls, and hills. The state is ideal for trekking, especially in areas like Netarhat, Dalma Hills, and Parasnath Hills. Rock climbing and rappelling are popular around rocky landscapes like Rajrappa and Hundru Falls. Wildlife safaris in Betla National Park and Dalma Wildlife Sanctuary offer close encounters with elephants, deer, and other wildlife. Boating and water sports are also available at places like Dimna Lake and Maithon Dam.
